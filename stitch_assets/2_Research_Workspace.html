Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company’s business, financial condition and operating results can be affected by a number of factors, whether currently known or unknown, including but not limited to those described below. Any one or more of such factors could directly or indirectly cause the Company’s actual financial condition and operating results to vary materially from past, or from anticipated future, financial condition and operating results.

Macroeconomic and Industry Risks

The Company’s global operations are subject to complex and changing economic, political and regulatory conditions. Global economic conditions could materially adversely affect the Company. The Company’s operations and performance depend significantly on global and regional economic conditions.

[1]

Supply Chain Vulnerabilities: The Company relies on component suppliers, manufacturers and logistical services provided by third parties, many of whom are located outside of the United States. Substantially all of the Company’s hardware products are manufactured by outsourcing partners that are located primarily in Asia. A significant concentration of this manufacturing is currently performed by a small number of outsourcing partners, often in single locations. The Company has also outsourced much of its transportation and logistics management. While these arrangements may lower operating costs, they also reduce the Company’s direct control over production and distribution.

[2]

Furthermore, international trade disputes, changing geopolitical dynamics, and new or anticipated changes in regulations, including export controls and tariffs related to semiconductor technologies, could adversely affect the Company’s supply chain. The cost, quality and availability of components could be negatively impacted by such geopolitical factors, resulting in delays or increased costs.
